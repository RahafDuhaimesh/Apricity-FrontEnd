Who can adopt?

In addition to being found suitable and eligible to adopt by USCIS, prospective adoptive parents seeking to adopt a child from Jordan must meet the following requirements:
1- Age of Adopting Parents: The guardian father must be 35-55 years old and the guardian mother 30-50 years old.
2- Marriage: Generally, Jordanian law stipulates that all prospective parents for legal guardianship must be Muslim, married for five or more years, and medically certified as infertile in order to obtain legal guardianship in Jordan. In select situations, and on a case- by- case basis, non-Muslims may be granted guardianship/custody of a non-Muslim child by a juvenile court order issued through the civil court.Single women, but not single men, may be able to acquire legal guardianship of a female child.
3- Single nursing women may be permitted to acquire legal guardianship of male children under the age of two.
4- Minimum Income: The guardian’s employer(s) must provide detailed information about their income and employment status, as explained below.
5- Other requirements: The prospective family for legal guardianship may have no more than two children, including the child(ren) proposed for the legal guardianship. If the prospective guardians already have one child, then the Jordanian child being considered for legal guardianship must be of the same sex as the first child in the home. Prospective guardian parents who have previously obtained legal guardianship in Jordan must wait a minimum of two years before seeking legal guardianship of another child. Prospective guardians who are U.S. citizens may also want to review the general information on Adoption of Children from Countries in which Islamic Shari'a law is observed. Each child must be taken care of as if they are a biological family member.

Who can be adopted?

1- Eligibility for adoption: Jordan permits legal custody for purposes of adoption in the U.S. Under Jordanian law, a child is considered to be abandoned if the child is placed in the care of the Ministry of Social Development (MSD) and (1) the parents are unknown or (2) the child is born out of wedlock. There is no relinquishment in Jordan. The MSD considers all Jordanian children under its care as abandoned.
2- Abandonment: The MSD considers all Jordanian children under its care as abandoned. Under Jordanian law, a child is considered to be “abandoned” if the child is placed in the care of the MSD and (1) the parents are unknown OR (2) the child is born out of wedlock.
3- Relinquishment: There is no relinquishment in Jordan. The MSD considers all Jordanian children under its care as abandoned
4- Age of Adoptive Child: MSD may give legal guardianship for a child younger than18 years old. Please note that for a child to meet the definition of an orphan under U.S. immigration law, a Form I-600, Petition to Classify Orphan as an Immediate Relative, must be filed on the child’s behalf while the child is under the age of 16 (or under the age of 18 if the child is the birth sibling of another adopted child who meets the age requirements and immigrated or will immigrate as an orphan based on adoption by the same adoptive parent(s)). Please see the USCIS website for special rules on filing dates for children aged 15-16 or siblings aged 17-18.
5- Sibling Adoptions: Sibling legal guardianship exists in Jordan.
6- Special Needs or Medical Conditions: Children with special needs or medical conditions are eligible for guardianship.
7- Waiting Period: The period of waiting for an available child depends on the childs availability, and takes an average of one year.

How to Adopt?

1- Visit the school’s Social Development Directorate to submit a request for incubation
2- Conduct a home social study of the family structure.
3- The completed study of the documents, conditions and placements shall be submitted to the Incubation Department if the Ministry has not completed the approval by His Excellency the Minister.

Documents

1- A summons from the couple explaining their desire to embrace.
2- A medical report proving the inability to have children.
3- A disease-free certificate for both spouses.
4- A proof of Islam from the Sharia court for non-Muslim families.
5- A certificate proving the nature of the spouses work and the amount of the familys income.
6- No convictions for both spouses.
7- A copy of the passport/family book of the spouses.
8- A copy of the marriage contract.
